TICKET #001 Resolved

VolleyConnect — AI Chatbot

AI Lab Artifact · Built with Claude

A conversational assistant designed for someone who just moved to a new city and wants to find a place to play volleyball. Given a location, it suggests how to locate nearby courts and how to find out who typically plays there and when.

The design choice I'm most proud of isn't a feature — it's a constraint. I deliberately kept the assistant from inventing specific court names, addresses, or schedules it couldn't verify, and instead had it point users to sources they could actually check: park district sites, Meetup or Facebook groups, and gym class schedules. It's easy to make a chatbot sound confident; a genuinely useful one has to know the limits of what it can claim. That instinct — don't state something as fact unless you can back it up — comes directly from years of writing ticket resolutions that other technicians had to trust.
→ Try VolleyConnect
TICKET #002 Resolved

Musubi — Hyperlocal Skill-Sharing App

App/Product Design Project · Built with Claude Code

A full landing page and product concept for a marketplace that connects neighbors offering skills — cooking, tutoring, repairs, lessons — with people who need them right now, the same way a rideshare app surfaces a driver. Users can browse who's free nearby, or flip on "Free Time Mode" to make themselves instantly bookable.

Where VolleyConnect was about designing a single conversation, Musubi was about designing a whole system: onboarding, trust signals, browsing, and booking all had to work together without a real backend behind them yet. The piece I'm most proud of is the three-step booking flow — see who's free → book on your terms → meet, rate, repeat. Collapsing a marketplace's usual friction (search, negotiate, schedule, pay, trust) into three plain steps took more iteration than the visual design did. It's the same instinct from IT support: the fix isn't done until the person using it doesn't have to think about how it works.
→ Try Musubi
